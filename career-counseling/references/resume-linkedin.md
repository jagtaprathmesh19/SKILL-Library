# Resume & LinkedIn Deep Reference

## Resume Review Protocol

### Step 1: Structural Audit (30 seconds)
- Format: Reverse-chronological? (Required for ATS)
- Length: 1 page (<10 yrs exp) or 2 pages max
- File type: PDF? (always recommend PDF)
- Font: Readable (Calibri, Garamond, Georgia — 10–12pt body)
- Margins: 0.5–1 inch; no columns (kills ATS parsing)
- No: photos, graphics, icons, tables, text boxes, headers/footers with key info

### Step 2: Content Quality Audit
For every bullet, ask: Does this prove impact, or just list a duty?

**The XYZ Formula (Google's standard):**
> "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]"

**The CAR Formula:**
> Challenge → Action → Result

**The Before/After Test:**
- Before: "Responsible for managing client accounts"
- After: "Managed 22 enterprise accounts ($4.2M ARR), achieving 97% retention and upselling 6 accounts totaling $380K in new revenue"

### Step 3: Keyword Optimization (ATS)
- Pull 8–10 exact phrases from the job description
- Embed naturally in bullets and summary (not a keyword dump section)
- Match verb tenses: present for current role, past for previous
- Common ATS killers: headers/footers, tables, columns, text boxes, images

### Step 4: Summary Section Rewrite Formula
The summary should answer in 3–4 lines:
1. Who are you professionally (title + years)
2. What's your superpower / specialty
3. What impact do you drive
4. What are you targeting next (optional but powerful)

**Weak summary:**
> "Marketing professional with 6 years of experience looking for new opportunities in a dynamic company."

**Strong summary:**
> "B2B SaaS marketing leader with 6 years driving demand gen at scale — grew pipeline 3x at two Series B startups through content-led SEO and ABM programs. Seeking a VP Marketing role at a growth-stage company entering enterprise."

### Step 5: Red Flag Checklist
Flag these and advise a fix:
- [ ] Employment gaps over 3 months — need brief explanation in cover letter or interview, not resume
- [ ] Job hopping (3+ roles in 3 years) — group contract/consulting work; add "contract" label
- [ ] Outdated tech or skills — remove tools >7 years old unless specifically required
- [ ] Generic objective statement — remove entirely or replace with strong summary
- [ ] "References available upon request" — delete, it's 1990
- [ ] First-person pronouns ("I managed...") — remove, implicit
- [ ] Responsibilities instead of accomplishments — rewrite
- [ ] Missing dates or fuzzy date ranges — add months + years

## LinkedIn Profile Optimization

### Headline (220 chars)
Don't just use your job title. Use your value proposition.

**Formula:** [What you do] | [Who you help] | [Outcome you drive] | [Optional: seeking X]

**Weak:** "Marketing Manager at Acme Corp"
**Strong:** "B2B SaaS Marketing Manager | Demand Gen & ABM | Helped 2 startups scale from $1M → $10M ARR | Open to VP roles"

### About Section
- Length: 1,500–2,000 characters (not max 2,600 — leaves it feeling stuffed)
- Tone: First-person, conversational, not a third-person bio
- Structure:
  1. Hook (1–2 sentences — lead with your most compelling thing)
  2. What you do and who for
  3. Your track record (2–3 achievements with numbers)
  4. What you're passionate about / what drives you
  5. Call to action: "Open to conversations about [X] — feel free to reach out"

### Experience Section
- Mirror resume bullets but allow more narrative
- Add context: team size, company stage, broader impact
- Pin 1–2 posts or media items to each role if you have them

### Featured Section
Use it. Add:
- A portfolio piece or case study
- A published article or talk
- Your resume (PDF) for easy downloading
- A strong recommendation or award

### Activity & Visibility
- Connection base: 500+ connections dramatically improves search visibility
- Post cadence: 1–2x per week (text posts outperform links in most feeds)
- Engage: Comment thoughtfully on 3–5 posts/day from target companies or industry leaders
- Creator Mode: Turn on if you post regularly — unlocks analytics and "Follow" button

### Profile Photo & Banner
- Photo: Professional, clear face, neutral/clean background, smile — worth the investment in a real photo
- Banner: Use it. Brand yourself. A simple branded image beats the default blue 100% of the time.

## Cover Letter Guide

When to write one: Always, unless the application explicitly says optional AND it's a high-volume role.

**Structure (3 short paragraphs + close):**

1. **Opening hook** — Don't start with "I am applying for..." Start with why you care about *them*.
   > "When [Company] launched [product/initiative], I immediately thought — this is exactly the kind of problem I want to work on."

2. **Your fit** — 2–3 specific reasons you're the right person. Mirror their language from the JD.

3. **Their gain** — Flip it: what will they get from hiring you? One concrete example.

4. **Close** — Confident, not begging. "I'd love to discuss how my background in [X] maps to the [specific challenge]. Happy to make time this week."

**What NOT to do:**
- Rehash the resume verbatim
- Say "I am a team player with strong communication skills"
- Write more than one page
- Make it about what you want ("This role would help me grow...") instead of what you offer
